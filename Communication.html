Communication

Communication is a vital component of any workplace, and it plays a critical role in ensuring the smooth and efficient functioning of an organization. Effective communication is essential to building and maintaining positive relationships between employees, teams, and management, and it helps to ensure that everyone is working towards the same goals

Non-verbal communication accounts for a significant portion of human interaction. When communicating solely through text, we lose a great deal of nuance. This requires you to be mindful of what you say and how you phrase things, as well as sensitive to what others say.

If we improve our communication methods and adhere to general guidelines, we will be able to increase our productivity significantly.

Please ensure that you have read the communication guidelines so that you understand what is and is not acceptable.

Text Based Communication

Several methods of text-based communication are in place. Using text messages has many benefits. The first benefit is a readily accessible record of past debates and resolutions. This is helpful for those who can't make it to a meeting, as well as for those who want to review the meeting's past actions at a later time

For Text based communication, we use Slack. Slack is a popular communication and collaboration platform used by businesses and teams of all sizes. One of the main advantages of using Slack is that it provides a centralized platform for communication, allowing team members to collaborate, share information, and work together more efficiently. With Slack, users can create channels for different projects or topics, and communicate through direct messages or group conversations. Slack also supports the integration of other tools and services, such as file sharing, task management, and video conferencing. This integration allows team members to easily access the tools they need without having to switch between multiple applications. Overall, Slack can help teams streamline their communication and improve their productivity, making it an effective and valuable tool for businesses of all types Alternative is Semsto Chat, which also help to communicate based on project or individual one

Voice and Video Chat

For voice or video chat we can use skype, zoom and google meets. For Internal communication we will use Skype and for communication with clients we will use zoom or google meet as per convenient of client

In-Person Meetings

In-person meetings provide numerous benefits for team members working on a project or task. Firstly, face-to-face communication enhances the clarity and understanding of ideas and reduces the potential for misunderstandings that can occur through virtual communication. Body language, tone, and nonverbal cues can all be observed, leading to a deeper and more meaningful connection between team members. In-person meetings also foster a sense of team spirit and community, allowing team members to build trust, relationships, and camaraderie that can improve collaboration and productivity. Finally, in-person meetings provide the opportunity to brainstorm and ideate together, leading to better and more creative solutions. Overall, in-person meetings allow for a more engaging, interactive, and productive experience for team members, leading to improved outcomes and higher levels of satisfaction.

We will use Semsto for scheduling all meetings. After each meeting, the MOM will be sent to all participants.

1. Phone Use Policy

The following policy outlines the acceptable use of personal and company- owned phones in the workplace. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and visitors

Acceptable Use:

  1. Personal phone use is permitted during non-work hours and during designated break times.

  2. Company-owned phones must be used for work-related tasks only.

  3. Personal phone use during work hours is only permitted in emergency situations.

  4. Texting, social media use, and other distracting activities on personal or company-owned phones are prohibited during work hours.

  5. Company-owned phones must be kept in a secure location when not in use.

  6. Employees should not use their phones to take pictures or record video in the workplace without prior authorization.

Un-Acceptable Use:

  1. Employees should not share confidential or sensitive information through personal or company-owned phones.

  2. Employees should not use company communication channels for personal purposes.

  3. Employees should not save confidential or sensitive information on personal or company-owned phones without prior authorization.

Personal Devices:

  1. Personal devices used for work-related tasks must be registered with the IT department.

  2. Employees should not use personal devices to access sensitive information or company data.

  3. Employees are responsible for the security and privacy of their personal devices.

Enforcement:

  1. Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

  2. Employees are responsible for reporting violations of this policy to their supervisor or the HR department.

Lost or stolen company-owned phones must be reported immediately to the IT departmen.

All level L1 employee are allow to keep phone with them and rest of the team member have to put phone in phone wallet. During working hour use of any kind of social media or entertaining or news site is not allowed and strictly prohibited. We strictly restricted phone use during working hours but in case of emergency can attend phone by taking permission of supervisor.

This phone use policy is designed to ensure a productive and professional work environment, protect sensitive information, and maintain the security of company-owned phones and personal devices. Employees are expected to comply with this policy and to use personal and company- owned phones in a responsible and appropriate manner.

2. Guidelines

Effective communication is essential for a productive and collaborative work environment. The following guidelines outline the acceptable use of communication channels in the workplace. These guidelines apply to all employees, contractors, and visitors.

Email:

  1. Use professional language and tone in all email communications.

  2. Include a clear and descriptive subject line.

  3. Be concise and to the point.

  4. Use a clear and easy-to-read font and formatting.

  5. Avoid using all caps, exclamation marks, or other unnecessary emphasis.

  6. Review and proofread the email before sending.

Instant Messaging:

  1. Use professional language and tone in all instant messaging communications.

  2. Use the appropriate messaging app for work-related communication.

  3. Use a clear and descriptive message to begin the conversation.

  4. Be concise and to the point.

  5. Use appropriate emojis and GIFs to express emotion or tone.

  6. Avoid using informal language or slang.

  7. Respond promptly to messages.

Phone Calls:

  1. Use a professional tone and language during phone calls.

  2. Use an appropriate greeting when answering the phone.

  3. Identify yourself and your company when making calls.

  4. Be prepared with any necessary information or documents before making a call.

  5. Avoid using the phone for personal calls during work hours.

  6. Use appropriate voicemail messages and call-back times.

In-Person Communication:

  1. Use professional language and tone during in-person communication.

  2. Pay attention to non-verbal cues, such as body language and facial expressions.

  3. Avoid using informal language or slang.

  4. Be respectful and courteous to colleagues and visitors.

  5. Be mindful of noise levels and interruptions in the workplace.

Enforcement:

  1. Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

  2. Employees are responsible for reporting violations of this policy to their supervisor or the HR department.
